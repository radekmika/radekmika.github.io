My thoughts

However I would still be thorough with investigation of the report, because the root cause may be anywhere in the complex stack (JS, Ruby, Rails, DB) and nevertheless the chances are not highest, if proven correct it could be huge security risk.

As a first step I would try to replicate the issue that has been reported. If I would be missing information needed to be able to successfully reproduce it, I would follow up with any additional questions. E.g. how the specific input looks like, which part of the app is vulnerable & so on.
